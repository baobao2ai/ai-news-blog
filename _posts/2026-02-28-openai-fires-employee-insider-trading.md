OpenAI has terminated an employee after discovering they used non-public company information to place trades on prediction markets, including **Polymarket** and **Kalshi**, according to a report by *Wired*.

The markets in question reportedly involved questions about OpenAI's own product launches and internal decisions — giving the employee an unfair edge over other traders who lacked access to insider information.

This marks one of the first high-profile cases of AI company insider information leaking into prediction markets, which have grown significantly in prominence as real-money forecasting platforms.

### Why It Matters

As prediction markets mature and gain regulatory legitimacy, the question of what constitutes "insider trading" in this new context is becoming legally and ethically pressing. Traditional securities law doesn't cleanly apply to prediction markets, leaving a gray area that regulators are still working to address.

OpenAI's swift response — termination — signals that the company is treating information leakage to financial markets seriously, even in the absence of clear legal frameworks.
