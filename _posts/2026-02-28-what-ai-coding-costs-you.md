A detailed post by developer Tom Wójcik is making the rounds on Hacker News (62 points and climbing), titled *"Finding the Right Amount of AI"*. It's a candid breakdown of the real costs of integrating AI coding assistants into a software development workflow.

### Key Points

- **Financial costs** add up fast: at heavy usage, API costs for code generation can exceed the value delivered, especially for small projects.
- **Cognitive debt**: over-relying on AI-generated code means understanding less of your own codebase, increasing long-term maintenance burden.
- **The sweet spot**: AI is most cost-effective for boilerplate, tests, and documentation — not for core architectural decisions.

> *"The cost isn't just dollars. It's the attention you stop paying to your own code."*

The post resonates with a growing counter-narrative in the developer community that AI coding tools, while powerful, require intentional use to avoid dependency traps.

### Discussion Highlights

Commenters on Hacker News noted that the calculus changes dramatically based on team size, project complexity, and whether you're using local models vs. cloud APIs.
